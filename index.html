How It Works

Competition Details

Create Your Product

Students create an original product and build a slideshow presentation covering all required components:

  • Function: What does your product do?
  • Problem Statement: What problem does it solve?
  • Visual Representation: Diagram, 3D model, or physical model
  • Limitations: Honest assessment of shortcomings
  • Competitive Advantages: Why your product wins

Present to the Sharks

Teams present their slideshow to a panel of real-world finance professionals. Each slot follows a strict format:

  • 4 minutes — Presentation
  • 2 minutes — Judge Q&A
  • 6 minutes total per team

Advance to Finals

The top team from each panel qualifies for the finals round, where the stakes get higher. See the Finals section below for full details.

The Final Round

Finals Format

Qualification

How You Get There

The top team from each panel advances to finals. Only one team per panel moves on — make your panel presentation count.

Format

Curveball Questions

Finals consist of two curveball questions. Each poses a real-world business scenario — a competitor's move, a product failure, a market shift — and asks for your team's response.

Timing

4 Minutes Per Question

Each team gets approximately 4 minutes total to prep and answer each question. Judges expect detailed, well-reasoned responses — think fast, speak clearly.

Strategy

Know the Pool

You won't know which two questions you'll face in finals, but upon registration you'll receive the full list of all 4 possible curveball questions to prepare in advance.

Pro Tip

Preparation Is Your Edge

Since you receive all 4 possible curveball questions at registration, the teams that win finals are the ones who prepare thorough, specific responses for every scenario. Don't show up winging it — the judges won't.
